Rowing,"a sport which involves propelling a boat on water using oars"

Rowing is the act of propelling a boat through the water with the use of oars. In a rowing shell, the rower sits on a seat that moves both forward and backward. This enables the rower to bend and straighten his or her legs increasing the length of each stroke.

Types of rowing

The sport has two basic types of rowing: sweep rowing and sculling. In sweep rowing, athletes hold one oar with both hands while in sculling, the athletes have two oars, one in each hand.

The types of boats

There are eight types of boats in the sport which can be customized or changed at one's consent and can contain an extra person which is called the Coxswain who is responsible for steering the boat, safety reasons, coordinating the power and controlling the rhythm of the rowers. Coxswains are always present in larger boats but sometimes aren't present in smaller boats to lessen the overall weight of the boat

The ergometer,"a machine measures work or energy used during a period of time"

An ergometer is a machine that is the most commonly used for rowing on land and is translated into "a measure of work"

Type of Erg Description Example
Water resistance erg This erg contains a paddle revolving in an enclosed tank of water. The mass and drag of the moving water create the resistance similar to being on the water
Air resistance erg This erg consists of a fan flywheel in a container and when a rower pulls on the handle, it causes the flywheel to spin and create wind. This resistance is variable due to the fact that the user controls the resistance level by how hard they row.
Magnetic Resistance erg This erg uses a magnetic brake system that can be moved to change resistance. Magnetic resistance ergs produce no friction and are almost virtually silent
Air and Magnetic Resistance Erg This erg has a blend of both air and magnetic resistance. Rowers have the ability to adjust magnetic tension and also has the ability to row faster and feel the added air resistance.

The Rowing tank

A rowing tank is an indoor facility which attempts to mimic the conditions rowers face on open water. Rowers sit in fixed rowing positions, with a channel of water to either side of the 'boat'. Rowing tanks are primarily used for off-season rowing, muscle-specific conditioning, and technique training, or when bad weather does not allow for open water training.

How to row

Rowing technique contains the catch, the drive, the finish and the recovery. The catch is the initial part of the stroke, The drive is where the power is generated while the finish is the final part of the stroke. Then, the recovery is the initial phase to begin taking a new stroke.These phrases are repeated until a duration of time or distance is completed

The Catch: This is when the knees are bent with the shins in a vertical position. The back should be roughly parallel to the thigh without leaning too far and the arms and shoulders should be extended forward and relaxed.

The Drive: This is when the legs are extended but the body remains in the catch posture. As the legs continue to full extension, the rower engages the core to begin the motion of the body levering backward. When the legs are flat, the rower begins to pull the handle toward the chest.

The Finish: The legs are at full extension and flat while the arms are in full contraction with the elbows bent and hands against the chest.The back of the rower is still maintained in an upright posture.

The Recovery: The recovery is a slow slide back to the initial part of the stroke with the actions are in reverse order of the drive. It gives the rower time to recover from the previous stroke.
